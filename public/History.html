What Started the Renaissance?

The Renaissance stemmed from the revival of classical wisdom and knowledge from Greek and Roman texts, the rise of humanism and the invention of the printing press. Namely, it was said that the term “revival” came from the revival of interest for human potential and classical learning instead of medieval scholasticism. An Italian poet, Francesco Petrarch, was the one who rediscovered these ancient texts (e.g. Cicero) containing different concepts such as humanism. What is the Renaissance?

Following the Middle ages was The Renaissance, otherwise known as the period of “rebirth”, started in Florence and was a time in European history that emphasized on the advancements on art, sciences and philosophy. Taking place from in the 14th to 16th centuries, it marked a time where people started to move away from religion-based reasoning and worldview and moved toward the idea of humanism and human potential. Because of this, the artists and thinkers took inspiration from translated ancient Greek and Roman texts, which made significant progress in expression and learning. What made Renaissance art thrive?

Art in the Renaissance prospered through the crucial patronage of wealthy families in Florence (such as the Medici Family). They funded artists that gave them the resources to continue creating and experimenting. Over time, artists introduced new and different techniques that focused on perspective and proportion that contributed to the depth and realism of the art style.
