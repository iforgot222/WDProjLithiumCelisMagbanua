Art Styles: Cangiante

Cangiante

Cangainte is derived from the Italian word cangiare meaning “to change.” This painting mode is characterized when a painter paints in a different hue when the original hue cannot be made darker or lighter. This technique utilizes a concept of color theory when some hues naturally are lighter or darker than others because of their values. For instance, if a painter is painting in green or yellow, they may utilize blues or violets (colors with the darkest hue) as shadows because there is simply not a darker yellow or green color available. This, consequently, creates a vibrant iridescent composition that shifts hues in light and dark planes

What are values?

Notice how red, blue, and violet seem ”darker” than colors like yellow or cyan despite all colors being fully saturated? Values (light or dark) come naturally with hues.
