Art Styles: Sfumato

Sfumato

Derived from Italian word sfumare, meaning to “tone down” or “to evaporate like smoke” (Brittanica, n.d.). It is an art technique that utilizes indistinct and fine shading that creates a smoky or soft effect on the plane where light meets dark. Paintings that use sfumato are characterized by soft gradients for shading instead of lines or borders (e.g. crosshatching in disegno). Example: The Nativity, also known as The Holy Night (La Notte) by Correggio.
