Four Canonical Painting Modes

Cangiante

Cangiante is a painting technique characterized by the use of different colors to depict shadows and highlights, rather than relying solely on light and dark tones.

Chiaroscuro

Chiaroscuro is a painting technique that involves the use of strong contrasts between light and dark to create a sense of volume and three-dimensionality.

Sfumato

Sfumato is a painting technique that involves the delicate blending of colors and tones to create soft transitions between light and shadow, resulting in a smoky or hazy effect.

Unione

Unione is a painting technique that combines elements of both sfumato and chiaroscuro, using vibrant colors and smooth transitions to create a harmonious effect.

Disegno v.s. Colorito

Disegno

Italian word for “drawing”, priority of illustration, sketching, and drawing over colourism. Example: Study for the Disputation of the Holy Sacrament: twenty clerics and ecclesiastics discussing (1483-1520) by Raphael.

Colorito

Italian word for “colouring”, priority of tone, pigments, and hues in painting. Example: Sistine Chapel Ceiling: The Creation of Adam (1508–1512) by Michelangelo.
