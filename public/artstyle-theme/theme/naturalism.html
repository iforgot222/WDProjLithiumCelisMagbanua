Art Themes: Naturalism

Naturalism

In Naturalism, artists and painters prioritized depicting the natural world with life-like accuracy, especially with human anatomy (with nude subjects). Within this art theme, realistic anatomy, mastery in perspective, and precise shadows were praised. However, aside from depicting humans, painters commonly focused on nature as a subject from the rise of humanism during the renaissance.

But what is the difference between Naturalism and Realism?

While both styles focus on depicting reality, they differ in the philosophy that they convey in art. Art under Naturalism typically emphasized on nature, landscapes, and people (in terms of anatomy) while art under Realism emphasized on the hardships of the working class during the Renaissance (focusing on the philosophy realism). Additionally, Realism was later popularized in the 19th century in response to the Industrial Revolution.
