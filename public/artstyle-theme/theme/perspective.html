Art Themes: Linear Perspective

Linear Perspective

Linear perspective was thought to b first employed in 1415 by Italian architect Filippo Brunelleschi then later developed by renaissance artists later in time. It is a system characterizedd by the illusion of depth and three-dimensionality on a flat plane by having invisible orthogonals (parallel lines) converge in a single vanishin point locared on the horizon line of a composition.
