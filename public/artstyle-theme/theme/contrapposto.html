Art Themes: Contrapposto

Contrapposto

(derived from “counterpoise”) This art theme is commonly referred to a type of pose in classical and Renaissance art wherein a standing person shifts their weight onto one leg, so that their hip rises in the opposite direction. This creates an effect that emphasizes the natural curvature on the body with the sense of relaxed theatricality. Additionally, this style was firstly adopted by Kouros sculptures of Ancient Greece but was revived late in the classical era. Example: Birth of Venus by Sandro Botticelli.
